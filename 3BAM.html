Material analysis at Bundesanstalt für Materialforschung

My first encounter with flame retardants was at the federal research institute of material research and testing, Berlin, where I characterized various materials. Particularly bio-polymers were the most interesting to me. I performed mechanical tests and tests with respect to their flame-retardant properties and analyzed the data. Examples are the investigation of the minimum oxygen concentration required to sustain a flame and determining the polymer’s heat release capacity; it was the beginning of a firerytale!

My two favorite projects were studying the long-term effects of weathering conditions on the polymer structure properties and the study of fire behavior of flame retardants from bio-resources.

  • Limiting Oxygen Index, LOI
  • Attenuated Total Reflectance-Fourier transform Infrared Spectroscopy, ATR-FTIR
  • Thermogravimetric Analysis-Evolved gas analysis, TGA/TGA-FTIR
  • Cone calorimeter
